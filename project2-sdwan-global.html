Project Overview

A Fortune 500 telecom company needed to transform its global wide-area network (WAN) from traditional MPLS to Software-Defined WAN (SD-WAN) technology. The goal: reduce network costs by 40%, improve application performance, and enable faster cloud adoption across 800+ carrier locations spanning North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia.

As Senior Project Manager, I owned vendor management, program execution, coordinator training, and financial tracking for this multi-year, multi-million dollar transformation.

The Challenge

  • Scale & Complexity: 800+ locations across 4 continents with different network architectures, vendor ecosystems, and regulatory requirements
  • Team Coordination: Managing 20+ field engineers across USA and APAC zones with different timezones, availability windows, and skill levels
  • Resource Scheduling: Engineers were juggling multiple projects — needed a way for them to self-assign to sites based on timezone and availability
  • Speed vs. Quality: As the program accelerated, manual scheduling became a bottleneck. We needed to deploy faster without sacrificing quality
  • Financial Tracking: Invoicing was tied to completed sites — needed real-time visibility into progress for accurate billing

My Approach & Solution

The Innovation - Self-Service Scheduling Tool: As the program scaled and team size grew, I identified that manual scheduling was our bottleneck. I built a custom Microsoft Teams scheduling tool that allowed engineers to:

  • View available sites by region and timezone
  • Self-assign based on their availability and preferences
  • Automatically sync with project tracking systems
  • No more back-and-forth emails or missed assignments

Data-Driven Dashboards: The scheduling tool fed real-time data that we used to build dashboards showing:

  • Deployment progress by region and timeline
  • Sites completed (for invoicing purposes)
  • Deployment velocity trends
  • Bottlenecks and risks

Vendor Management & Training: Coordinated with multiple vendors (Cisco, Viptela, Fortinet) to ensure technical alignment. Trained 15+ project coordinators on SD-WAN architecture, deployment procedures, and vendor escalation protocols.

Financial Governance: Established automated invoicing based on completed sites tracked in the dashboard — reducing billing disputes and improving cash flow accuracy.

Key Outcomes:

  • Successfully deployed SD-WAN across all 800 locations on schedule and on budget
  • Scheduling tool reduced manual coordination by 80% — freed team to focus on actual deployments
  • Real-time dashboards enabled accurate invoicing and executive visibility
  • Achieved 40% network cost reduction (primary business goal)
  • Trained 15+ coordinators who became core PM resources for future projects
  • Established a repeatable global deployment playbook used for subsequent enterprise programs

Key Learnings

  • Automate Your Bottlenecks: When manual processes became the constraint, I built a tool instead of hiring more people. That mindset is core to scaling programs efficiently.
  • Data Enables Decision-Making: The dashboards didn't just track progress — they revealed bottlenecks early and informed vendor negotiations.
  • Empower Your Team: Self-service scheduling gave engineers autonomy and ownership. Engagement improved, and execution accelerated.
  • Document & Replicate: Built a playbook from this program that became the foundation for future global deployments.
